Clara Vergès

Observational cosmologist, specialised in instrument modeling and data analysis for Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) polarisation experiments.

I am currently a Harvard Postdoctoral Fellow at the Center for Astrophysics in Cambridge (USA), within the CMB group. Before that, I was a PhD student at APC in Paris, where I defended my thesis in September 2020.
I am a member of the BICEP/Keck collaboration, as well as an associate member of the LiteBIRD satellite project. I was previously a member of the POLARBEAR/Simons Array and Simons Observatory collaborations.

Research interests

My research work focuses on studying the signal of the CMB to look for a specific polarisation pattern: the cosmological B-modes. The detection of this signal would be a smoking gun for inflation theories, allowing us to better understand the very beginning of the Universe. I mainly focus on developping better instrument models, to improve the way we design, calibrate and analyse data.

Instrument modeling

The detection of the very faint signal of the cosmological B-modes requires an accurate modeling of instrumental effects. I participate in developping ab initio models for future CMB polarisation experiments, based on most recent hardware development.

Design & Calibration

Instrument models are used in the design phase to anticipate and forecast the impact of various hardware effects, and possibly mitigate them in the design. Once the instrument is deployed, it must be carefully calibrated and characterised to ensure it achieves the required scientific goals.

Component separation

One of the main challenges in our quest for B-modes is to be able to separate the CMB signal from Galactic contaminations - thermal dust emission and synchrotron radiation. This step, known as component separation, requires accurate models of both the instrument, and to the extent possible, of Galactic foregrounds.
